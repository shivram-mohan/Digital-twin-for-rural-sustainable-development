# ICAR Guidelines: Groundnut Cultivation in Anantapur

## Sowing
- Recommended varieties: TMV-2, K-6, ICGS-76, TAG-24
- Sowing window: June 15 - July 15 (after 50mm cumulative rainfall)
- Seed rate: 100 kg/ha for spreading, 120 kg/ha for bunch types
- Spacing: 30cm x 10cm

## Water Management
- Critical irrigation stages: Pegging (35-40 DAS), Pod formation (60-70 DAS)
- Protective irrigation at pegging increases yield by 25-30%
- Total water requirement: 450-500 mm
- Soil moisture should not fall below 25% during critical stages

## Fertilizer
- Basal: 20 kg N + 40 kg P2O5 per hectare
- Gypsum: 200 kg/ha at flowering (40-45 DAS)

# PM-KUSUM Scheme Summary

## Component A: Decentralized Solar Power Plants (up to 2 MW)
- Farmers can install on barren/fallow land
- Power purchased by DISCOM at fixed tariff, 30% CFA

## Component B: Standalone Solar Agricultural Pumps
- Solar pumps up to 7.5 HP
- Subsidy: 30% CFA + 30% State + 40% farmer (loan available)

## Component C: Solarization of Grid-Connected Pumps
- 30% CFA + 30% State subsidy
- Excess power sold to DISCOM

# Jal Jeevan Mission - Rural Water Supply
- Target: 55 LPCD for every rural household via tap connection
- AP Status: ~65% households connected (2024)
- Groundwater: Anantapur classified Over-Exploited in several mandals
- Recommended: Rainwater harvesting, check dams, farm ponds

# Anantapur Climate Profile
- Annual rainfall: 544 mm (61% SW monsoon)
- Most drought-prone district in AP
- Temperature: 20-42°C range
- Soil: Red sandy loam (78%), low water retention
- Solar: 5.5 kWh/m²/day, 250-280 clear days/year

# PMFBY (Crop Insurance)
- Premium: 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi
- Covers: Drought, flood, hailstorm, pest/disease
- Notified crops for Anantapur: Groundnut, Sunflower, Castor, Chickpea
- Claim process: Report within 72 hours via app or agriculture officer
